# WORLD-HAPPINESS-ANALYSIS
**BY Ezichi Williams John**

## Overview

This dashboard presents a detailed visualization of the World Happiness Report from 2015 to 2019, covering 169 countries across 11 global regions. It highlights trends in happiness, life expectancy, GDP per capita, and regional differences to provide a clear understanding of global well-being patterns.

## Objective

The objective of this dashboard is to analyze global happiness indicators, compare regional performance, identify high- and low-performing areas, and highlight the major factors influencing happiness scores around the world.

## Key Metrics
- Average Healthy Life Expectancy  
- Total GDP per Capita  
- Average Happiness Score  
- Total Number of Regions  
- Total Number of Countries  
- Average Score per Region  
- Average GDP per Capita  
- Yearly Life Expectancy Trend  

## Insights and Findings

- Regions such as Australia & New Zealand, North America, and Western Europe consistently record high happiness scores.  
- Regions including South Asia and Sub-Saharan Africa score lower, indicating gaps in economic strength, healthcare access, and social support.  
- Happiness shows a strong relationship with GDP per capita, demonstrating how economic stability contributes to life satisfaction.  
- Life expectancy declines between 2015 and 2017 but increases again by 2019, reflecting improvements in global health conditions.  
- Countries such as Finland, Denmark, Norway, and Switzerland consistently rank among the highest in overall happiness.

## Recommendations

- Strengthen healthcare systems to improve life expectancy in lower-performing regions.  
- Support economic policies that encourage income stability and job creation.  
- Invest in social support systems and community development to enhance well-being.  
- Adopt strategies and structures from consistently high-performing countries.  
- Develop long-term plans focused on stability, infrastructure, and public welfare.

## Conclusion

This dashboard presents a clear view of global happiness trends between 2015 and 2019. The findings highlight the importance of economic strength, healthcare quality, and social support systems in determining overall happiness levels. The insights provide a strong foundation for further analysis and decision-making.
